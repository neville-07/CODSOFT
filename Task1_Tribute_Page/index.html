Jensen Huang

Founder & CEO of NVIDIA

About Jensen Huang

Jensen Huang, born in Tainan, Taiwan, is a visionary entrepreneur and the co-founder and CEO of NVIDIA Corporation. After moving to the United States at a young age, he went on to earn a degree in electrical engineering from Oregon State University and later completed his master's from Stanford University. In 1993, Huang co-founded NVIDIA, a company that would go on to reshape the tech world through groundbreaking advancements in graphics processing units (GPUs), artificial intelligence (AI), and deep learning.

What sets Jensen apart is not just his technical brilliance, but his ability to predict and shape the future of computing. Under his leadership, NVIDIA transformed from a graphics card company into a global powerhouse behind the AI revolution, self-driving technology, and even healthcare innovations powered by deep learning. He’s known for his sharp mind, clear vision, and down-to-earth attitude despite running one of the most valuable tech companies in the world.

Life & Achievements

  • 1963 – Born in Tainan, Taiwan
  • 1973 – Moved to the U.S. at age 10, later studied electrical engineering at Oregon State University
  • 1984–1992 – Worked at LSI Logic and AMD while building a strong reputation in the chip industry
  • 1993 – Co-founded NVIDIA with just $40,000 and a vision to revolutionize graphics computing
  • 1999 – Released the first GPU, the GeForce 256, branding it as the “world’s first GPU”
  • 2006 – Launched CUDA, allowing GPUs to be used for general-purpose computing — a massive shift in the tech world
  • 2010s – Led NVIDIA into AI, data centers, and autonomous vehicles — expanding far beyond gaming
  • 2023 – Under his leadership, NVIDIA hit a $1 trillion valuation during the global AI boom

What I Admire About Him

  • Jensen’s long-term vision has always been ahead of its time. When others saw GPUs only for gaming, he saw the future of AI and science. That takes courage and clarity few leaders have.
  • I admire how he built something world-changing from almost nothing — starting NVIDIA with just a handful of people, and turning it into a company that powers everything from ChatGPT to self-driving cars.
  • He communicates with clarity, humility, and confidence. Whether it’s at GTC keynotes or interviews, he keeps things real and insightful, without unnecessary hype.
  • His work ethic is inspiring — he’s known to work long hours, stay hands-on with product development, and never lose curiosity, even after 30 years in the game.
  • As a student in cloud and AI technologies, I genuinely look up to him. His journey motivates me to stay curious, keep learning, and aim for impactful innovation.
